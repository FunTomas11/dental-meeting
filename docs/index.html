edsa
EDSA (European Dental Students’ Association) is an international organization representing over 70,000 students of dentistry in Europe. Twice a year, all EDSA delegates meet to jointly create new and improve existing projects. In addition, we discuss the integration of dentistry into society. The main goals of EDSA are to inform students about European dental organizations and policies, to support students' cooperation in their projects and to defend their interests, to develop and promote an international network of student exchanges, and to encourage national dental student associations to cooperate at an international level.
ptss
Polish Association of Dental Students (PTSS) is an organization associating students of the medical and dental faculty throughout Poland. The scope of our activities includes scientific activities, organizing training for students and preventive campaigns aimed at educating the public about oral health.

The PTSS branch at the Medical University of Lublin has almost 220 members. We pay particular attention to promoting proper oral hygiene among children and adults, but also to personal education and gaining experience during the workshops we organize. We have already organized our flagship National Scientific Conference Denteast twice - in 2016 and 2020, and each of them was very popular.
lublin Lublin is the largest Polish city east of the Vistula River, it has a population of nearly 340 000. As the largest eastern city it serves an important cultural and historical role. A must-see is definitely the National Museum of Lublin which is one of the oldest and largest museums in eastern Poland. Also an extremely important part of Polish history is contained in Majdanek State Museum which brings back its’ visitors to the World War II. The city has a remarkable Old Town with cobbled streets and medieval layout. Historic buildings, both renovated and not, create a unique atmosphere. Lublin is known as a “Polish Capital of Festivals”, the most significance hold “Carnaval Sztukmistrzów” and “Night of Culture” It is also the seat of 5 public universities and several private higher education establishments. It means that the city is a home for many young people who maintain a youthful spirit of Lublin.

How to get here

We recommend you to take a plane to Warsaw - there are 2 airports:

  • Warsaw Chopin Airport
  • Warsaw Modlin Airport

From the first one you can transfer to the city centre by taxi (use bolt or uber app or regular taxi services) or by bus - numbers 175, N32 (if you are travelling at night).

To transfer from Warsaw Modlin Airport you can use taxi service but we would recommend travelling via train (Masovian Railway) to the city centre. From Warsaw Central Railway station you can easily get to Lublin by train in just 2,5 hours. Make sure you pick your destination as “Lublin Główny”. To see the schedule of trains and buy a ticket visit: https://www.intercity.pl/en/
